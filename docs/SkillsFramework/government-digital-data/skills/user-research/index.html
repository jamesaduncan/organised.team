  • User Research

    • User research involves planning, conducting, and analysing research to understand user behaviours, needs, and motivations through observation techniques, task analysis, and other feedback methodologies.

    Proficiency Levels

    1. Awareness

      You can understand the value of user research and recognise when user research insights should inform decisions.

    2. Working

      You can conduct basic user research activities like interviews and usability testing, and interpret findings to inform design decisions.

    3. Practitioner

      You can design and execute complex research studies, synthesise insights from multiple research methods, and present findings to influence strategic decisions.

    4. Expert

      You can lead research strategy, establish research capabilities across teams, and champion evidence-based decision making throughout the organisation.
