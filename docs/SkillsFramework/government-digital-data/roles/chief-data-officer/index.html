  • Chief Data Officer

    • A chief data officer provides strategic leadership for data across the organisation. They are responsible for maximising the value of data assets, establishing data governance, and driving data-led innovation to support organisational objectives.

    Typical responsibilities

    • Define and execute organisation-wide data strategy and vision
    • Establish comprehensive data governance frameworks and policies
    • Lead cultural transformation towards data-driven decision making
    • Build and manage high-performing data teams across the organisation
    • Drive innovation in data science, analytics, and emerging data technologies
    • Ensure compliance with data protection regulations and ethical standards
    • Influence executive leadership on data investment and strategic decisions
    • Represent the organisation in external data leadership forums

    Required Skills

    • Strategic Thinking Expert
    • Leadership and Guidance Expert
    • Data Governance Expert
    • Data Strategy Expert
    • Stakeholder Relationship Management Expert
    • Communicating between the technical and non-technical Expert
    • Data Ethics and Privacy Expert
    • Innovation and Emerging Technology Practitioner

    Career progression

    This is typically a senior executive role

    Indicative Civil Service grades

    • SCS2 (Senior Civil Service Pay Band 2)
    • SCS3 (Senior Civil Service Pay Band 3)
