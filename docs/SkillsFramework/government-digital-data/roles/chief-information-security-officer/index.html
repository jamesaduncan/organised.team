  • Chief Information Security Officer

    • A chief information security officer provides strategic leadership for information security across the organisation. They are responsible for protecting the organisation's information assets, managing cyber security risks, and ensuring compliance with security regulations.

    Typical responsibilities

    • Define and execute organisation-wide information security strategy
    • Establish comprehensive security governance frameworks and policies
    • Lead incident response and cyber security crisis management
    • Build and manage security teams and capabilities
    • Drive security awareness and culture transformation
    • Ensure compliance with security regulations and standards
    • Manage relationships with security vendors and external partners
    • Report security posture and risks to executive leadership

    Required Skills

    • Security Strategy Expert
    • Leadership and Guidance Expert
    • Risk Management Expert
    • Security Expert
    • Governance Expert
    • Stakeholder Relationship Management Expert
    • Communicating between the technical and non-technical Expert
    • Incident Management Practitioner

    Career progression

    This is typically a senior executive role

    Indicative Civil Service grades

    • SCS1 (Senior Civil Service Pay Band 1)
    • SCS2 (Senior Civil Service Pay Band 2)
